  2. Med School Specialization and Starting Salary

    Question: How does the amount of student loan debt affect the specializations that medical students choose?

    In recent years, there has a been a trend in medical school of hyper-specialization, by which I mean no one goes into primary care practice anymore. This article from June 17, 2013 details the issue. There are many possible factors that could affect this trend such as: student loans when starting medical school, average cost of medical school, current interest rates, average length of residencies, average length of fellowships, and starting attending salary. I want to crunch the data and see if there is a correlation between student loan debt and choosing a highly-paid specialty.

  3. Mental Health and Urbanism

    Question: What affect does living in an urban environment have on mental health?

First, due to the horrible NYC winter I have just experienced, I began to look into the number of sunny days in major metro areas throughout the country. I want to continue this by looking into rates of depression within these cities.
